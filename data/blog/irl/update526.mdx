# So a Lot has Happened

Looks like I left off around February? So around that time I... wasn't doing too great. Not any worse than any other day or month than I had been for the last 2 years, mind you. I was not in a good place. I hadn't been in a good place for virtually all of my adult life, and a good long while before then.

Add to that a nearly drained bank account and I was looking at the prosepect of being forced out of my own life and back into my parent's house. Needing to reconsider my choice of career. It was a frightening prospect.

So I did something crazy: I decided to prioritize my mental health and enter into a program that could offer some professional help. Not one on one therapy, but a group program. For several hours every week day, I attended this program, learning techniques to manage my depression and partook in group therapy sessions, and it unquestionably was a great help to me. While it would have been nice if they had simply "fixed" me (as I initially thought they would), the hard truth is there is no easy answers or shortcuts. Your brain is your own. The engine that makes you. So instead what I got were tools to help service that engine.

Also SNRIs. I always figured if I needed to be on meds someone trusted in my life would speak up and tell me. Turns out that's not true. They just treated it as natural here. It's been... different. Honestly, I don't know what effect they are actually having on me. I just resolved to trust the program, and that means trusting the meds. It does suck though that when I forget, I get a crippling headache. I've gone my whole life without developing a crippling coffee addiction, only to have a vice maybe worse forced upon me.

Withdrawl symptoms every day unless I get my fix.

# But More Importantly...

Incredibly enough, on the cusp of ruin, I did find a new job. With the help of some friends (truly the best way to get ahead, like it or not), I have been granted the chance... one last chance... to break back into programming (profressionally).

So yeah, I'm working at the Fed[eral Reserve Bank] as an SDET now. I've been hesitant to talk too much or promote it because after more than 2 years, it felt like a dream that I didn't trust myself not to wake up from.

But here I am, a week and half into it... and I still can't really believe it's real. Tbh, I can't believe I am not going to cock it up somehow. But this time, it's not just my butt on the line. Good people put their names and reputations on the line because they saw something in me. It might be a bit messed up that I can fight harder for my friends than I can for my own survival, but... whatever helps?

Also, it's weird, but I have to point it out: a LOT of my anxiety and depression disappeared overnight when I suddenly had a job that could pay my bills. I'm sure it's a coincidence.
